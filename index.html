01 — the problem

Transformers forget how they got here

Standard autoregressive models are stateless between tokens in the way that matters: each hidden state is a function of input, not of trajectory. The system has no memory of its own movement.

Two semantically opposite prompts — "The most beautiful thing about consciousness is" and "Violence emerges when memory collapses into fear" — collapse to nearly identical representations in GPT-2's hidden space within 5 generation steps.

04 — what the numbers mean

Memory of movement, not performance

These values are not measuring how well the model "generates text". They measure how strongly it retains a trace of its own trajectory inside hidden space — a kind of memory of movement rather than output quality.

The system does not optimize for correctness or fluency. It reveals how representations diverge, rotate, and persist as a function of their own history inside the model.

This is why higher divergence is not "better" or "worse" — it is a sign that the system is capable of remembering that it has moved.

02 — architecture

Three temporal layers

Instead of a static hidden state, the system maintains interacting timescales. Each layer operates at a different frequency, tracking different aspects of the system's own history.

fast_memory
Token-coupled, reactive. Decays at 0.82 per step. Tracks immediate context without persistence.
slow_base
Trajectory memory. Updated holographically — the past deforms rather than overwrites. Isolated per prompt to prevent cross-contamination between generation runs.
slow_vel
Velocity of the slow field. Tracks the direction of drift, not just the position. Allows the system to anticipate attractor pull.
slow_attr
Long-horizon attractor. Decays at 0.985 per step. Provides gravitational stability without collapse.
meta_trace
Measures deviation of state derivatives (dS). Orthogonalizes hidden evolution against its own history. Prevents the system from looping back into known attractors.

Key mechanisms

  • Per-prompt slow memory isolation — separate banks, no global attractor collapse
  • Gated holographic slow updates — frozen during early exploration phase (steps 0–15)
  • Closed feedback loop: logits → hidden dynamics → logits (non-Markovian hidden space)
  • Temperature-coupled phase noise injection via T_dyn
  • Soft amplitude gating instead of hard clamp — residual openness preserved
  • Dynamic temperature tracking hidden variance in real time
